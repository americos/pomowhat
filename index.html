First things first: What is the Pomodoro Technique®?

It's an awesome time management technique to take the best out of your time and get things done. It was created by Francesco Cirillo.

Pomowhat? What the heck is a Pomodoro?

Everytime we say a "Pomodoro" we reffer to: "A full 25 minute work session", that means: NO distractions, NO interruptions, NO BS, just get to work!

A pomodoro is an atomic entity (more on this later, but in a nutshell... it can't be divided) that help us to focus on a task at a time. It also the means "Tomato" in Italian.
Mama Mia!

What you will need:

  1. A Timer
  2. A Todo List
  3. A pen or pencil

P.S. You can use as a Timer: a physical timer, a Pomodoro software, your watch, your phone or whatever works for you. (Tips on timers later on)

5 Simple Steps to Start. Bring It!

  1. Choose a task to complete
  2. Set your Pomodoro timer to 25 mins
  3. Start you pomodoro and work for 25 mins straight: GO!
  4. When the time is over put a check mark in your TODO List next to the current item you are working on. Take a 5 minutes break, enjoy.
  5. At the end of the break start a new Pomodoro, if this is your fourth pomodoro, take a longer break (20 minutes for example)

What are the benefits of using this technique?

  • You will get things done.
  • You know how long it took you to do XYZ Task. (You just need to count the amount of check marks in your todo list, sweat!)
  • Eventually you will be able to give a better estimate of how long a task will take you (Think of: "This task will take roughly... 6 pomodoros")
  • You will be able to focus on work for periods of 25 minutes and then you have 5 minutes to do whatever the hell you want. Read your mail, watch a video on you tube, tweet or Stalk your ex-girlfriend on facebook if you want too.

Bottomline... There are many benefits and trust me with time you will discover even more.

Wait so focus for 25 minutes without distracting yourself?

Anyone could do almost anything for 25 minutes: You can run, walk, talk, eat, read for 25 minutes right?

According to Parkinson's Law:

Work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion.

Just think of the last time your boss ask you do fix that problem in 1 hour... you maybe put on your Niccolo Machiavelli mask and did whatever you had to do to get it done.


Niccolo Machiavelli

Same thing applies here... In 25 minutes you can do a lot a stuff. You just need to focus.

What should I do If I get interrupted?

  1. Put a mark on your TODO List: A Star * will do, this means that during this task you were interrupted. This voids the Pomodoro
  2. Reset you pomodoro, when interrputed the Pomodoro get's invalidated (Remember when we said a Pomodoro is Atomic? Well think about this like an all or nothing thing)
  3. Next time try to avoid/prevent these interruptions (Check the ninja techniques against interruptions down below)

We all suffer form interruptions, how can I handle them?

First let's agree on something... There are 2 types of interruptions:

1) The ones you can control:

Turn off you email notifiers, stop tweeting and checking facebook. Don't worry your followers and fans will be there at the end of the pomodoro.

2) The ones you can't:

A phone call, your boss screaming at you, a coworker asking you to check and error on this code, The marketing guy knocking in your cubicle to let you know there are just a few donuts left, etc.

Now, you have the power over the ones you can control. Just close for now that social network webpage, email notifier, etc.

The ones you can't control it's a little bit more tricky more on this in the next section

Interruptions you can't control? Try this:

  • Let you clients, coworkers, boss, etc. know that you are trying/testing a new productivity technique call "The Pomodoro Technique" and so you won't be available to answer that email, chat message, etc, right away
  • Resist the temptation of answering emails right away. DON'T WORRY: if its an urgency they will call you.
  • If somebody is pinging you every minute, tell him politely that you need to get "in the zone" so your are sorry but can talk right now.
  • Put on your headphones and play that awesome song that you love hearing while working.

Bottomline If your office is on fire or the SWAT Team is entering your CEO's office next door... OK then stop what you are doing, place a note of what you were currently doing to cancel that pomodoro. Then keep calm, breath and run to your nearest exit!

Should I use The Pomodoro Technique for every task in my day?

NO

Don't use it for activities where you are having fun and you don't have a deadline. For instance don't use it for:

  • Spending time with your girlfriend, boyfirend, kids, family, similar other, etc.
  • Reading, cooking, Walking your dog and other leisure activities.
  • Practicing sports, going to the gym, etc.

You get the idea... I suggest you should use it when you have to get something done and/or you have a deadline.
