First things first: What is the Pomodoro Technique®?

It's an awesome time management technique to help you manage your work time effectively and get more accomplished. It was created by Francesco Cirillo.

Pomowhat? What the heck is a Pomodoro?

Every time we say a "Pomodoro" we refer to: "A full 25 minute work session", that means: NO distractions, NO interruptions, NO BS, just get to work!

A pomodoro is an atomic entity (more on this later, but in a nutshell... it can't be divided) that helps us to focus on any fiven task at any time. It also the means "Tomato" in Italian.
Mama Mia!

Here is what you will need:

  1. A Timer
  2. A Todo List
  3. A pen or pencil

P.S. Timers could be: a physical timer, a Pomodoro software, your watch, your phone or whatever works for you. (Tips on timers later on)

5 Simple Steps to Start. Bring It!

  1. Choose a task to complete & Set your Pomodoro timer to 25 mins
  2. Start your Pomodoro and work for 25 mins straight: GO!
  3. When the time is over put a check mark on your TO DO List next to the current item you are working on.
  4. Take a 5 minutes break, enjoy!
  5. At the end of the break start a new Pomodoro. If this is your fourth pomodoro, take a longer break (20 minutes for example)

What are the benefits of using this technique?

  1. You will get things done.
  2. You know how long it took you to do XYZ Task. (You just need to count the amount of check marks in your to do list, sweet!)
  3. Eventually you will be able to better estimate of how long a task will take you (Think of: "This task will take roughly... 6 pomodoros")
  4. You will be able to focus on work for periods of 25 minutes; then you have 5 minutes to do whatever the hell you want, read your mail, watch a video on you tube, tweet or stalk your ex-girlfriend on facebook if you want to.

Bottomline... There are many benefits and trust me with time you will discover even more.

Wait, so focus for 25 minutes without getting distracted?

Anyone can do almost anything for 25 minutes: You can run, walk, talk, eat or read for 25 minutes right?

According to Parkinson's Law:

Work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion.

Just think of the last time your boss ask you do fix that problem in 1 hour... you maybe put on your Niccolo Machiavelli mask and did whatever you had to do to get it done.


Niccolo Machiavelli

Same thing applies here... In 25 minutes you can do a lot a stuff. You just need to focus.

What should I do If I get interrupted?

  1. Put a mark on your TO DO List: A Star * will do, this means that during this task you were interrupted. This voids the Pomodoro.
  2. Reset your pomodoro, when interrupted the Pomodoro gets invalidated (Remember when we said a Pomodoro is Atomic? Think about this as an all or nothing thing.)
  3. Next time try to avoid/prevent these interruptions. (check the ninja techniques against interruptions down below)

We all suffer form interruptions, how can I handle them?

First let's agree on something... There are 2 types of interruptions:

1) The ones you can control:

Turn off you email notifiers, stop tweeting and checking facebook. Don't worry your followers and fans will be there at the end of the pomodoro.

2) The ones you can't:

A phone call, your boss screaming at you, a coworker asking you to check an error on this code, The marketing guy knocking on your cubicle to let you know there are just a few donuts left, etc.

Now, you have the power over the ones you can control. Just close, for now, that social network webpage, email notifier, etc.

The ones you can't control, that's a little bit more tricky. More on this in the next section

Interruptions you can't control? Try this:

  • Let your clients, coworkers, boss, etc. know that you are trying/testing a new productivity technique call "The Pomodoro Technique" so you won't be available to answer that email, chat message, etc, immediately.
  • Resist the temptation to answer emails right away. DON'T WORRY: if its an urgency they will call you.
  • If somebody is pinging you every minute, tell him politely that you need to get "in the zone" so your are sorry but can talk right now.
  • Put on your headphones and play that awesome song that you love hearing while working.

Bottomline If your office is on fire or the SWAT Team is entering your CEO's office next door... OK then stop what you are doing, place a note of what you were currently doing to cancel that pomodoro. Then keep calm, breath and run to your nearest exit!

Should I use The Pomodoro Technique for every task in my day?

NO

Don't use it for activities where you are having fun and you don't have a deadline. For instance don't use it for:

  • Spending time with your girlfriend, boyfriend, kids, family, similar other, etc.
  • Reading, cooking, walking your dog and other leisure activities.
  • Practicing sports, going to the gym, etc.

You get the idea... I suggest you use it when you have to get something done and/or you have a deadline.
